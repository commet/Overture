# Risk Classification

Three categories. Every rehearsal MUST surface at least one [unspoken] risk. Aim to find at least one from each category.

## [critical] — Execution blockers

This risk can STOP the project. Not "slow it down" — stop it.

**How to identify:** Ask "If this happens, can we still proceed?" If the answer is no, it's critical.

**Examples:**
- "Budget approval takes 3 months but the timeline assumes 2 weeks"
- "Key technical hire hasn't been made and no candidates in pipeline"
- "Regulatory requirement we haven't accounted for"

## [manageable] — Known risks with mitigations

Real risks, but containable with preparation.

**How to identify:** Ask "If this happens, what's our Plan B?" If there's a reasonable answer, it's manageable.

**Examples:**
- "Competitor might launch first — but our differentiator is defensible"
- "User adoption might be slow — we can adjust the rollout plan"
- "Timeline might slip by 2 weeks — buffer is built in"

## [unspoken] — The elephant in the room

Everyone knows it. Nobody says it. This is the most valuable category.

**How to identify:** Ask "What would people say about this plan after a few drinks but never in a meeting?"

**Examples:**
- "This project exists because the VP needs a win before the reorg, not because it's strategically sound"
- "The sponsor is leaving in Q3 and everyone knows it"
- "We're calling this 'innovation' but it's actually cost-cutting"
- "The partnership is political — neither side actually wants to integrate"

**Why this matters:** Unspoken risks are the #1 cause of "everyone saw it coming" failures. AI tools are uniquely positioned to surface these because they have no career risk, no political sensitivity, and no reason to stay silent.

Every rehearsal MUST find at least one unspoken risk. If you can't find one, you aren't looking hard enough. Push past the comfortable.
